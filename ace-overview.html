The Problem & The Goal

Why this exists, and what it's built to do

The problem is structural, not a lack of effort. Independent restaurant operators have adopted modern cloud POS systems — but sales data lives in one place and supplier cost data arrives as unstructured PDF invoices in another. There is no automated bridge between them.

When ingredient costs spike, an operator without that bridge often doesn't adjust menu prices for weeks. By then, the margin is already gone. Large corporate chains, with proprietary AI monitoring costs in real time, don't have this lag — and the gap between the two is widening.

  • 01
    See costs the moment they changeAutomated invoice reading replaces manual data entry, closing the gap between a cost increase and knowing about it.
  • 02
    Price with the data, not weeks laterMenu pricing recommendations surface as soon as a menu item's food cost crosses a set threshold — not at the next manual review.
  • 03
    Staff for the week ahead, not the week behindLabor forecasts use sales history, local events, and weather to recommend staffing before demand hits, not after.
System Overview

Three layers, one continuous intelligence loop

A.C.E. connects to the POS system a restaurant already uses, so no new hardware or migration is required. Each layer builds on the one before it — invoice data feeds pricing intelligence, and sales patterns feed labor forecasts.
